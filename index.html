The facehugger is the second stage of the xenomorph’s life cycle, and is, at its core,

a delivery system for inserting the xenomorph’s DNA into a host. The facehugger isn’t so

much about delivering sperm, like real spermatophores are, but is instead used to deliver a knot

of cells (basically an embryo) through the mouth of the host. It executes this goal by clinging

to the host’s face and tightening its tail around the throat to minimize oxygen moving to

the brain, keeping the host alive but unconscious. (It also apparently delivers

“a cyanose-based paralytic chemical” to help sedate the host.) Once the ‘Morph DNA

has been delivered via a proboscis down the throat, the facehugger detaches and dies.
